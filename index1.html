Introduction

The project on this page provides an overview of the final project we did for our STATS 141B class. This project is done by a group of 3 students: and Janet Loyola Stephanie (Hiu Man) Lam, Chloe (Jieyi) Chen,

Each of us in our group have a passion for traveling. That's how we came up with the idea of helping tourists discover the most outstanding places in California by focusing on three categories: hotels, restaurants, and landmarks.


Our goal of the project is to provide a travel guide for California visitors by showing them the best hotels, restaurants, and landmarks with the applications of web data processing, interactive data visualization, natural language processing, and interactive user input using Python.


We decided to provide travel guide by:

  • Showing best landmarks, restaurants and hotels in CA using different graphs
  • Second,asking users to input a city
  • Third, display a basic overview of the city (through paragraphs and dataframe)
  • Lastly, analyze the reviews for different businesses in CA using natural language processing

We followed these steps by breaking this project into four parts.

  • Data Wrangling
  • Query Database and Graphs
  • Getting basic information of each city.
  • Analyzing the review content.

Since we want our users to be able to track the different categories for their own personal needs, we used interactive plots to give the user a better way to gain information.

    Restaurant Category

    Since there are many different categories in restaurants, we will only focus on the top 10 most frequent cateogries. From this bar plot, we can see that Mexican food is the most popular cuisine.
    Now, we need a plot to see how these categories are distributed in different ratings.
    We see that most of the restaurants have 4 stars rating or higher. Hence, we can tell that CA has pretty posh restaurants overall.
    In this map,The bigger dots indicate higher average review count. We see that most of the restaurants in different cities are between 4 and 4.5 stars. There is currently no city that has a average rating higher than 4.5.
    We want the users to be able to look at where are the top 3 restaurants are located by the cuisine they want. Therefore we created functions to extract the top 3 restaurants by cuisine and plot the locations on a map
    Most of the top 3 restaurants are located near San Francisco and the Los Angeles to San Diego area.

    The Hotel Category

    From the dataframe we got from yelp, the hotel category doesn't contain much varieties of category. Therefore, for better analysis of the hotel data, we first restricted the data to those that are indeed only hotels, and looked at the pie chart of the star distribution and map of average rating in each city.
    From the pie chart, we can see that the majority of hotels are between 3 and 4 stars. There is only 3.04% of hotels are rated 5 stars while 26.7% are 3.5 stars.
    Compared to the restaurants category, there are actually quite a lot of cities that have a average hotel rating between 1 and 3 stars. The only 2 red dots in the map: the dot in Exeter is very small and the average reviews count is only 14, indicating that even though it has a average 4.5 rating in the city, there might be just one hotel in the city. On the other hand, the hotels in Palm Spring is very nice because the average review count is very high.
    We grouped the hotels into different categories by finding the pattern within their name such as Hotel, Motel, or Inn. Same as the restaurant category, the map shows that most top hotels are located near San Francisco and LA-SD area.

    The Landmarks Category

    From the pie chart, most landmarks seem to have high rating, ranged from 4 to 5 stars. Since we focused on only the landmarks and historical buildings category and there is no pattern in their names, we will find and plot the top 10 landmarks in California instead.
    From the previous plot and this map, we see that landmarks have dramatically more cities that are highly-rated. And most of them also have high average review count.
    Different than the restaurant and hotel category, out of the 10 top landmarks, half of them are located in San Francisco while Los Angeles does not have any.
